Self-Parking Car Miniature


An independent project that aims to model the self-parking function of recent cars with a car miniature. This small robot-car should be able to detect whether there is enough space to park, how far it is from the curb, and then park itself close to the curb, without collision with other cars/objects and the curb.


There are one distance sensor in the front, one at the back, and two on the side (I'm only going to park the car on one side of the street). Of the two sensors on the side, the lower one will be to detect distance from the curb, and the higher one for distance from other cars/obstacles. There is a LED display to indicate different states of the car - finding parking space, parking, and parked.


More details on the design and the process of making the car miniature can be found on the project website.

Bunny Robot


HackHolyoke in April 2015 was right before Easter Day, so my teammates and I wanted to make a bunny robot that can go up to people and deliver some gift on this holiday.

The robot has 2 wheels attached to 2 servos, which are wirelessly controlled with a Myo gesture armband to move forward, rotate left and rotate right. The robot has 3 distance sensors, one in the front, one on the left and one on the right; these distance sensors can detect obstacles and stop the bunny even if the user's giving another command. When the user decides to stop the bunny (when the bunny reaches someone!), the bunny will say lovely words to that person through the RGB backlight display and deliver them some gift!

We also won HackHolyoke 2015 First Prize!

More details about the project on DevPost.

Work Time Tracker App


An Android app that helps users store time entries and produce meaningful reports so that users stay up-to-date with their current work hours and earnings. Targeted to freelancers whose schedule is not very consistent and the often work on multiple projects at a time so it’s hard to keep track of those projects separately.

More details about the project on GitHub.
